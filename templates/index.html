President Tinubu Bans Fresh Pepper in Nigeria

In a move that has sent shockwaves across the nation, President Bola Ahmed Tinubu has announced a nationwide ban on all fresh peppers. The unprecedented directive, issued in a late-night press conference that many initially mistook for a comedy skit, cites national security and economic stability as the primary reasons.

"For too long, we have been a people blinded by pepper. It is in our stew, our soup, our jollof. We cannot see straight! Our national decisions have been too hot, too hasty, too… peppery. Enough is enough. It is time for national coolness."
- President Bola Ahmed Tinubu

The ban, officially called the “National Anti-Irritant and Sensory De-escalation Bill (NAISD),” aims to promote a more "calm and reflective" national psyche. According to a presidential aide who spoke on the condition of anonymity while eating a lukewarm plate of rice and stew, the government believes the constant intake of pepper is responsible for the country’s high-pressure political instability and economic degradation.

The law prohibits the production, consumption and distribution of fresh pepper. Anyone caught in buying or selling of Fresh pepper will be liable to face 6 months imprisonment. The Department of State Services (DSS) reported that several "pepper-dealers" have already been detained and are awaiting trial.

In a final twist, the President’s last statement during the press conference hinted his intention to end the cries of Nigerians

"I want to ensure that the cries of Nigerians come to an end!. So tomorrow, we will ban Onions"
- President Bola Ahmed Tinubu
